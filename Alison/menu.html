Regular Fries
  • Classic thin-cut fries that are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
  • They are a timeless favorite.
  • They have a straight shape with squared-off ends.
  • They pair well with a variety of sauces and condiments such as ketchup, mayonnaise, or aioli.

Waffle Fries
  1. They are made by cutting potatoes into a crisscross pattern,
    resulting in a grid-like shape.
  2. They have a thicker cut and a distinct texture that holds sauces and toppings well.
  3. Crispy, lattice-shaped fries that resemble a waffle.
  4. They are commonly seasoned with salt or various spices and
    can be enjoyed on their own or as a side dish with a variety of dipping sauces.

Poutine
  • A Canadian specialty that consists of fries topped with cheese curds and smothered in gravy.
  • It creates a rich and savory combination.
  • The combination of the hot fries, melty cheese curds, creates a delicious and indulgent flavor profile.
  • Poutine has gained more popularity worldwide, and many variations now exist, incorporating additional
    toppings such as bacon, pulled pork, caramelized onions, or mushrooms.
